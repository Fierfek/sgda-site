Engineering Panel

Paul Laska
(Senior Generalist Programmer - Blind Squirrel Games)

Paul is a Senior Generalist Programmer currently working on an unannounced title for one of Blind Squirrel Games’ amazing partners. In his 2 years at the studio, Paul has contributed to shipping AAA titles and has lead a team of engineers on Sims 4. Past game projects Paul has worked on include Firefall, Wall-E, Transformers 1 & 2, Marvel Ultimate Alliance 2 and Spider-man 3, for Red 5 Studios, Savage Entertainment and Treyarch. Koray Hagen
(Senior Software Engineer - SIE Santa Monica Studio)

Koray is a tools and engine programmer currently working on central technology efforts and putting the finishing touches on the PlayStation 4 exclusive, God of War, at Santa Monica Studio. In his four years at the studio, Koray has developed an expertise in cutting-edge technology for game asset pipelines and character animation systems. In the past Koray has also contributed to Xbox, League of Legends, and Hearthstone at Microsoft, Blizzard, and Riot Games respectively. Having previously founded his own student game development club at Cal Poly Pomona, Koray enjoys sharing his experience with students.

Fundamentals of Branching Dialogue

Carrie Patel
(Novelist | Narrative Designer - Obsidian Entertainment)

Carrie Patel is a narrative designer and writer at Obsidian Entertainment, where she is currently Narrative Co-Lead on Pillars of Eternity II: Deadfire. Her writing credits include the Writers Guild of America-nominated Pillars of Eternity, the White March expansions, and Pillars of Eternity II: Deadfire. She is also a novelist and the author of the science fantasy Recoletta trilogy, which includes The Buried Life, Cities and Thrones, and The Song of the Dead. Her short fiction has appeared in PodCastle and Beneath Ceaseless Skies.

DESCRIPTION

Branching dialogue is a staple of many RPGS, and one that allows designers to present players with gameplay choices, offer options for players' personal expression, and create a stronger sense of a player-driven story. But authoring dialogue trees is complicated. Writers and designers must balance player freedom against the limits of scope, and they must present a consistent through-line even while offering players multiple avenues for navigating a dialogue. This presentation will cover the basics of branching dialogue: why it's a useful design feature, how to implement it well, and what to look out for.
