Questions about the SGDA Game Jam

Who can attend?
All students over the age of 18 are welcomed to attend! All participants must follow the GGJ Code of Conduct.
Is there a limit on registration?
Unfortunately, our venue only has enough space and we only have enough food to accommodate 200 people for UCR (Southern California site) and 100 people for UCSC (Northern California site). The earlier you register, the more likely you will be accepted, so sign up now!
What do I bring?
Please bring your student IDs in order to check in and any hardware or software you need to develop your game. Because this is an overnight event, consider bringing a pillow, sleeping bag, and anything else you need to make your stay comfortable.
Will there be food?
Yes! We will provide catered meals during the event, along with snacks and drinks.
Are there travel reimbursements?
Unfortunately, we do not support any form of travel reimbursements. Rest-assured, we are working hard on the quality of the event to make sure all attendees have a meaningful and great experience!
Are teams required?
You aren’t required to form a team prior to the event. However, we do want everyone to be on a team to work on an awesome game together during the game jam! We will have a social in the beginning of the event to help you get into a team if you don’t have one already.
What if I’m not very experienced?
No worries! We will have workshops at the start of the event for beginner jammers. Also, whether you need help on programming, art, music, or design, SGDA will have experienced student and professional mentors to guide you in the right direction. The Global Game Jam is a friendly, networking environment and a great way to gain more experience not only on game development, but also working in a team!

Questions about the SGDA Summit

What is the Summit and what can I do there?
The Summit is a single day gathering for any interested student, indie, or professional developers. Its purpose is to provide NETWORKING opportunities for all attendees while allowing them to also LEARN new skills and be inspired.
There will be guest SPEAKERS from a variety of different backgrounds including indie devs, AAA devs, engineers, artists, and businesspeople. Each of the host clubs will also provide WORKSHOPS on useful game dev skills and technologies. Whatever your profession is there will be something of interest to you.
There will also be opportunities to relax, play games and form connections with fellow game developers from across Southern California.
Oh, PLUS there will be giveaways, swag, and food!
Do I need to have experience making games?
NOPE! All you need is a passion for games and a desire to learn about game development. The Summit is geared toward learning so hopefully you will learn enough at the summit to get out there and make your own game!
How much does the Summit cost?
Parking will cost $4.00 for the day, but the event is FREE to enter! However, monetary donations to improve next year's Summit are appreciated.
What do I need to bring? What should I wear?
The summit is designed with the interest of learning and self-improvment in mind. We suggest you bring an open mind and something with which to take NOTES.
If you wish to participate in workshops, you should bring your LAPTOP as well. We will send out an email letting you know what software you should have installed beforehand, if any.
It will be a long day so dress SEMI-CASUALLY! Although industry professionals will be present, you should not treat the summit like a job interview. You may submit your resume when you register, but you do not need to bring it with you.
How do I get there?
Traveling from North / Los Angeles:
Take the 5S to the 10E, exit on Kellogg Dr.
Traveling from West / Orange County:
Take the 57N, exit on Temple Ave.

Traveling from South or East / San Diego or Riverside
Take the 15N to the 10W, exit on Kellogg Dr.

Nearest parking areas are shown on this map as well as vending areas where you can pay $4.00 for a one-day parking pass.
Why is there a 200-person limit for registration?
Unfortunately, our venue only has enough space and we only have enough food to accommodate 200 people. Members of clubs affiliated with SGDA will receive priority admission to the event. If you want to guarantee a spot at the event register as early as possible and consider becoming a member of one of the SGDA clubs.
After we reach our limit, students will still be able to register for "General Admission" but we will unfortunately be unable guarantee food, t-shirts, or seating at our events or general admission attendees.
Do I need to stay for the whole duration?
Nope! However, if you know for sure you will not be able to stay for the whole duration, please consider registering for "general admission" when EventBrite tickets go up. It is only fair to those who are able to enjoy the event to its fullest.
