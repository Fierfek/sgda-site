STUDENT GAMES SHOWCASE 2021

The Student Games Showcase is a segment of the SGDA Summit 2021 where we stream games made by students and invite developers to talk about them. All game teams of the Showcase can choose to opt-in to a mini-grant.

Submissions will be made to the itch.io page, found here.

Frequently Asked Showcase Questions


How will games be presented?

There are 2 options: a selected number of SGDA officers can play the game, or developers can play their own games on-stream. Either way, we will be asking developers questions about their process, what they’ve learned, and so on.

Does the build I submit have to be finished?

No, we don’t expect your game to be finished by April 25th (the deadline for submissions), but we do expect it to be playable enough for SGDA officers to judge. Bugs or unfinished content won’t be judged.

How many project members will be allowed to present for the Showcase?

The entire team is able to present during the Showcase. We expect presenters to follow the rules and guidelines of the server.

What happens if my game gets accepted?

Once we review submissions, we will coordinate with all applicants to choose a timeslot for their segment on either May 29th or May 30th. Other preparations include a tech check, request for further information and graphic(s), and a final build. Final builds will be accepted up to May 19th.
